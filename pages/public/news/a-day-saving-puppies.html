A Day Saving Puppies

June 8, 2026

By Alexandria Sofiakis

Share
Email

As I ran up the hilly road with my newly found friends in a land unknown to me, I was unprepared for what awaited us. “They arrested Wayne the second he stepped out of the car & refused to cite why” G said between breaths while running. “Did the Sheriff's department at least serve the papers he brought against Ridglan for them waving guns at people on public property?” M asked, “No” was the reply. “Fuckers, nepotism runs this place” M said angrily as we got to the top of the road. What we were facing looked like hell; wide open fields without cover, past that were trenches filled with sewage & waste from over 2,000 tortured dogs, after that was barbed wire which we were unsure if it was electrified or not, behind that a wall of 5ft hay bales, further behind those was the chain link fence surrounding the facilities, and even further still were countless hired crooks meant to defend this illegal facility from justice & us enforcing the law.

I saw the criminals already prepared, they had called for reinforcements & had already started deploying rubber bullets, pepper spray, & tear gas. None of us came with equipment for this, none of us were prepared for this. This was supposed to be a peaceful open rescue just like the ones I had seen before, after all everyone has a legal right to rescue animals from cruelty. What is illegal however is being part of a conspiracy to protect an evil & criminal private enterprise. One that steals your tax dollars for torture under the guise of “research” that fails more than 90% of the time, one that uses your tax dollars to arm these criminals, one that uses your taxes to shoot veterans & shove the elderly to the pavement. It was clear as I watched the so-called "Sheriff's Department” side by side with those violent criminals that they had been corrupted & could no longer be trusted to follow the law, quite the contrary they were now in open alliance with criminals against peaceful law abiding citizens.

Only part of our red team was there, we were supposed to have a training session beforehand but weather had canceled that opportunity the day before, and our leadership was hindered before we even got there by Wayne’s arrest. We had children & elderly on other teams, & everyone I spoke to was very green in terms of experiencing brutality at the hands of the state. Being in the protest scene for a while, I could tell my fellow rescuers had no idea what to do about a tear gas canister, what equipment we needed to deal with pepper spray, how to coordinate groups at separate side of the fence to push simultaneously, how to breach a chain link fence while facing rubber bullets. Most people wouldn’t have come all the way out here for a rescue operation, and absolutely everyone I knew before this day would’ve turned around & gone home at this point.

I’m not like them though, I charged straight into that open field knowing the odds were damn near 0 for success. The first tear gas canisters came our way halfway through the field & the small crowd I joined split, half went straight ahead before the cloud emerged & we went west of it to go around the back side of the facility. Back there immediately became worse, I could see from my periphery the “police” were coordinating with the security forces to box us who had gone to the back in, as unlike the over 1,000 allies we had up front we were less than 100 strong back here. There were no layers of defense standing between us & the chain linked fence back here, although the ponds in the back funneled us closer together. Instantly the tear gas started, I had no mask or goggles but I stayed with my allies there as they choked & fell. I managed to go past the cloud but I saw we had a downhill slope if we went along the fence further, so I turned around & regrouped with the others in the front while avoiding the “police” slowly closing their encirclement. All while these people they were brutalizing scream “We are unarmed & come in peace to enforce the law! Free the dogs!”.

Up front with bigger numbers we quickly got to work. First to go were people down in the stinky trenches with tools to cut the barbed wire. Next J brought a plank of wood wide enough to cross which I immediately did, then proceeded to help others make it across by relocating the hay bales to a more appropriate location (i.e. shove them in the trenches to build us a bigger bridgehead). They clearly underestimated our numbers, because they didn’t get any tear gas on this group at the front fence until after we had put half a dozen in the trenches. Our bridgehead was wide enough to avoid 1 canister of pepper spray, and we put our backs to the fence to breach it as the power tools were brought up. The grinding of the metal as that fence ripped open was nothing short of cathartic, god willing we were going to break the cages like we were breaking these chains. No sooner than 10 seconds after the grinding sound echoed across the fields we were drowned in tear gas & pepper spray with the full contingent of criminals converging on us.

Imagine feeling like you’re on fire as your skin chemically burns, your eyes on fire & unopenable, and you can’t breathe. Whatever you imagined from that image, imagine that but 100 times more painful, because that is what it felt like. I saw people laying face down in the trenches under barbed wire struggling to breathe as I desperately made my way back over the hay bale bridge. I had been in clouds of tear gas before but holding my shawl to my face was enough to sit through it, not this mix. I didn’t get a chemical sample but from my description my more chemistry inclined friends told me that was a special tear gas mix even the military isn’t allowed to use.

We tried to make several new breaches that day but each time with the same result. Despite that, I would gladly do it all over again. Because with over 1,000 people from all walks of life whom I had never met before that day I have a bond with them as strong as family. Because it could not be more clear who is on the side of good & who is on the side of evil. Because no being deserves to be forgotten & abused in a cage their entire lives.

Fortunately as time has since passed, it is clear what Ridglan & its supporters did that day was simply accomplish a Pyrrhic victory at best, if even that. I am happy to report 1,500 of the ~2,000 dogs have since been purchased, rescued, & are currently being relocated to the loving families these precious beings deserved from the start. Not only has ridglan been pressured in part by the open rescue attempt to surrender three quarters of the dogs they torture, but they are also facing a mountain of lawsuits for their boatload of illegal actions against open rescuers. That’s on top of the fact that what ridglan got in the end was monetarily significantly less than we offered during negotiations on the day of the open rescue attempt from my understanding. Serves them right, let this be a lesson to all abusers that bullying & violence will not save you from peaceful rescuers, it will only doom you.
