Vote by Mail Information

In Illinois, you can request a mail-in ballot up to 5 days before the General Election. Applications can be submitted online, by mail, or in person at your local election office. You do not need to provide a reason for requesting a mail-in ballot as this requirement was removed in 2009.

Mail-in ballot Application Deadlines and Instructions:

  • Deadline to Request: 5 days before Election Day
  • How to Apply: Online, by mail, or in person at your local election office
  • Return Method: Mail or drop off at designated locations

Completed mail-in ballots must be postmarked by Election Day and received within 14 days after the election to be counted.

For more information on how to request a mail-in ballot and important deadlines, visit the Illinois State Board of Elections website.

Early In-Person Voting Information

Early voting in Illinois begins September 24, 2026. You can vote at any early voting location in your county. Make sure to bring a valid ID with you when you go to vote early.

  • When: September 24, 2026 - November 2, 2026
  • Where: Any early voting location in your county
  • ID Requirements: Valid government-issued ID

For more information on early voting locations and hours, visit the Illinois State Board of Elections website.
